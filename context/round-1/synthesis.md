## Prose digest

**Verdicts.** Two `promote` candidates (p1, p3) and one `iterate` (p2). Two promotes in round 1 is unusual — the personas converged that both the broad state-machine framing (p1) and the narrow tool-routing framing (p3) are independently shippable, and they target different audiences (TS-native architects vs. solo vertical-agent builders). p2 occupies the middle ground but pays the scope tax for trying to be both a library AND a SaaS in 5 weeks; the iterate pivot strips it to OSS-only and pushes the hosted product to a v2 milestone, which all four personas agreed is the correct sequencing even where they disagreed on the score.

**What is converging.**
- **Runtime guard before type theater.** Pocock made this explicit on p1 and p3; Theo seconded it on p3. Both p1 and p3 mvp_scope now require the runtime guard to be proven first, with types layered on observable behavior.
- **Comparison framing on day one.** Theo demanded explicit `vs XState / LangGraph.js / Mastra` framing in the README for p1, and explicit `LangGraph alternative for TypeScript teams` framing for p2. Both are now in the proposals.
- **Migration / on-ramp story.** Addy on p1 (`wrapLegacyLoop`); Addy on p3 (concrete vertical demo); applied.
- **Drop premature integration.** Pocock and Theo both flagged `composeWithAgentAssert` as scope leakage in p3; removed.
- **Cut the SaaS for now.** Pocock, Theo, Addy, and (with reservations) Rauch all converged that p2's hybrid v1 ships nothing well; OSS-only with local replay is the unanimous pivot.

**What is still contested.**
- **Visualizer/platform gravity in p1.** Rauch (6) wants a visualizer or CLI in v1 because "a types-only library with no visualizer ships half the primitive." The applied pivot — a `tracestate dev` terminal CLI — is the smallest non-conflicting concession. Pocock and Theo will likely push back if v2 ambition creeps into v1 scope.
- **p2 SaaS sequencing.** Rauch's 8 was driven heavily by the hosted "PR-comment-shareable URL" viral loop. Cutting the SaaS to v2 lowers his enthusiasm but resolves the unanimous scope concern. This is the iterate verdict's central tension — the score spread of 3 points (Pocock 5 vs Rauch 8) is preserved deliberately rather than averaged away.
- **Vercel AI SDK lock-in is a cross-proposal architectural risk.** Both p1 (Pocock, Rauch) and p3 (Addy, Theo, Rauch — five persona-mentions across two proposals) flag the same brittleness: the AI SDK's tool shape changes between minor versions, and a narrowing layer built on top accumulates silent drift. p3 surfaces this most acutely because it is SDK-native by design; p1 has the option of treating the SDK as one adapter among several. **Cross-proposal decision the user must make before round 2:** does the agent-state-machine line stake itself to the Vercel AI SDK as the primary distribution surface (p1 + p3 in lockstep), or does it position the Anthropic SDK as a co-equal first-party target (p1) while p3 stays Vercel-coupled? The current pivots tilt toward the former for p3 (Vercel default) and the latter for p1 (Vercel default export but Anthropic peer adapter), which is internally coherent but should be a conscious user choice, not an emergent default.

**Convergence flag.** First round, no prior synthesis. Top mean score 7.25 is below the 8.0 single-criterion threshold; delta-from-prior is not computable. `converged: false`. Expectation for round 2: p1 and p3 should hold or rise after the pivots are absorbed; p2 either climbs into promote range with the OSS-only scope, or settles as a deliberately deferred idea.
